Derby Through the Ages

Roman Derby

Derby began as a Roman fort, established around 50 AD, known as Derventio. The Romans left their mark with a fort and a potential civilian settlement outside its walls.

Danish and Saxon Derby

The Danes founded the town of Derby in 873 AD, fortifying it. The town's name, "Derby," is derived from the Danish words "deor by," meaning "deer settlement."

Medieval Derby

During the Middle Ages, Derby thrived as a place of trade with various crafts, including a wool industry and leatherwork.

16th and 17th Century

Derby faced challenges during the 16th and 17th centuries, including the closure of monastic institutions and outbreaks of plague. However, its cloth industry flourished.

18th Century

In the 18th century, Derby grew as a market town, and industries like silk-making and porcelain production emerged.

19th Century

The 19th century brought significant changes, including gas lighting, the arrival of the railway, and the growth of various industries.

Derby in the 20th and 21st Century

In the 20th and 21st centuries, Derby continued to evolve with industries like manufacturing and tourism. The city remains a dynamic and thriving place.
